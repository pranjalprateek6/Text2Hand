What it does

A hand, not a font

01

Text becomes pages

Headings centred and underlined, lists indented, tables hand-ruled, figures boxed. Long documents spill onto new pages, up to eighty at a run.

02

PDFs come along

A PDF is read into Markdown you can correct before anything is written, because extraction is never perfect and a wrong heading should be a ten-second fix. Scanned PDFs go through local OCR.

03

Equations survive

Display maths is found by font signature, cropped from the paper, and traced back in as ink, fractions and product signs intact. Inline symbols are drawn glyphs: α β ψ φ Σ and friends.

04

Make it your own hand

Print the capture sheets, write them out, photograph them, and the extraction tools cut your writing into a font of its own. The bundled hand is real, and yours can replace it.

How it works

Three steps to paper

I

Type, or drop a PDF

The studio takes anything: notes, an essay, a whole paper. A PDF lands as reviewable Markdown first, equations excised as images.

II

Check it over

Extraction is never perfect, so the Markdown sits in the editor to be corrected. Pick paper, tilt and one of four pens.

III

Render and hand it in

Pages as PNG, the document as a PDF, or everything zipped. Every render is different: no two letters are the same image.

The hand

No two letters the same

This page was rendered from Markdown. Every mark on it comes from one real hand, captured from written sheets and cut apart, letter by letter and word by word.
