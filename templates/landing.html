What it does

A hand,
not a font.

01 Text becomes pages Plain text or Markdown in, page images and a PDF out. Headings centred and underlined, lists indented, tables hand-ruled, and long documents spill onto new pages up to 80 at a run.
02 PDFs come along A PDF is read into Markdown you can correct before anything is written, because extraction is never perfect and a wrong heading should be a ten-second fix. Scanned PDFs go through local OCR.
03 Equations survive Display maths is found by font signature, cropped from the page, and traced back in as ink, fractions and product signs intact. Inline symbols are drawn glyphs: α β ψ φ Σ and friends.
04 Four real pens Blue-black, black, blue and red, applied stroke by stroke with per-glyph pressure, so a page never reads as one flat colour.

The hand

No two
letters
the same.

This page was rendered from Markdown. Every mark on it comes from one real hand, captured from written sheets and cut apart, letter by letter and word by word.

Variants and jitterEach glyph gets its own rotation, size, wobble and ink pressure. Repeats never match.
Whole words in one strokeCommon words are written as single captured strokes, keeping the joins a real hand makes.
A hand that tiresWriting grows and loosens toward the foot of each page. Optionally it crosses out a word and rewrites it.
Paper that was scannedRuled lines, a red margin, warm grain, and a slight tilt, as if it came off a scanner.
