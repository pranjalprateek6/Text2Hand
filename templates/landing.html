02 / Method

Why it convinces

A font pastes the same letter every time. A person never writes one twice. Four things break that.

01 / Variance

No two the same

Every glyph gets its own rotation, scale, baseline wobble, spacing and ink pressure. Where several photographs of a letter exist, one is picked at random.

02 / Ligature

Whole words, one stroke

Common words are written as single captured strokes rather than assembled from letters, keeping the joins a real hand makes. About a third of any page.

03 / Fatigue

A hand that tires

Writing grows and loosens toward the foot of each page. Turn it on and the hand will start a word wrong, cross it out, and write it again.

04 / Artifact

Paper that was scanned

Ruled lines and a red margin printed before a word is written, warm grain, and a slight tilt. The rules skew with the writing, because they were on the page first.

03 / Evidence

A real page

Rendered from Markdown. Every mark on it comes from one hand, captured from written sheets and cut apart.
